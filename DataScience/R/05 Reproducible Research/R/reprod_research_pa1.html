Introduction

It is now possible to collect a large amount of data about personal movement using activity monitoring devices such as a Fitbit, Nike Fuelband, or Jawbone Up. These type of devices are part of the “quantified self” movement !V a group of enthusiasts who take measurements about themselves regularly to improve their health, to find patterns in their behavior, or because they are tech geeks. But these data remain under-utilized both because the raw data are hard to obtain and there is a lack of statistical methods and software for processing and interpreting the data.

Imputing missing value

The missing value is replaced by the mean for that 5-minute interval. A new histogram is plotted and mean and median is recalculated.

imputData <- data
imputData$steps <- apply(data, 1, function(x){
  if(is.na(x[1]))
    avgSteps$steps[avgSteps$interval==x[3]][1]
  else
    x[1]
})
imputData$steps <- as.numeric(imputData$steps)
sumSteps2 <- aggregate(imputData$steps ~ imputData$date, FUN=sum)
names(sumSteps2) <- c("date", "steps")
hist(sumSteps2$steps, main="Histogram on step taken each day")

The mean of the total number of steps taken per day (Missing value)

mean(sumSteps2$steps)
## [1] 10221.93

The median of the total number of steps taken per day

median(sumSteps2$steps)
## [1] 10395

After imputing missing data using the estimates, the new mean and median is smaller from that of the original data set.

    if(t=="Sunday")
      "Weekend"
    else if(t=="Saturday")
